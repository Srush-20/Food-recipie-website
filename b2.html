Chicken Kheema

Chicken kheema is a flavorful Indian dish made with minced chicken. It's typically prepared by sautéing minced chicken with a mix of aromatic spices, onions, garlic, and ginger. Tomatoes and various spices like cumin, coriander, turmeric, and chili powder are added to create a rich, savory sauce. This dish is often garnished with fresh coriander leaves and served with rice, roti, or naan bread. It's a popular choice for those who enjoy the flavors of Indian cuisine and is known for its spicy and satisfying taste.

prep time

10 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

4 servings

instructions

step 1

Cook the spaghetti or pasta according to the package instructions in a large pot of salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve about 1 cup of pasta cooking water and then drain the pasta.

step 2

While the pasta is cooking, in a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano cheese, and grated Parmesan cheese. Season with a generous pinch of black pepper. Set this mixture aside.

step 3

In a large skillet over medium-low heat, add the diced guanciale or pancetta. Cook until it becomes crispy and renders its fat, which should take about 4-5 minutes. If using garlic, add it to the skillet and sauté for an additional 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Remove from heat.

step 4

Once the pasta is cooked and drained, add it to the skillet with the cooked guanciale or pancetta (and garlic, if used). Toss to combine, ensuring that the pasta is coated with the rendered fat.

step 5

Quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the hot pasta and toss vigorously to combine. The heat from the pasta will cook the eggs and create a creamy sauce. If the sauce seems too thick, add a bit of the reserved pasta cooking water gradually until you reach your desired consistency.

ingredients

12 ounces (340 grams) spaghetti or other pasta of your choice

2 large eggs

1 cup (about 100 grams) grated Pecorino Romano cheese

1/2 cup (about 50 grams) grated Parmesan cheese

2 cloves garlic, minced

Salt and black pepper, to taste

Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish
