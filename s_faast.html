Sabudana Khichdi

Sabudana Khichdi is a popular and delicious Indian dish, especially enjoyed during fasting periods and as a breakfast or snack. It's made from sabudana, also known as tapioca pearls, and seasoned with a mix of spices and ingredients. Sabudana Khichdi is a favorite during fasting periods in India, especially during festivals like Navratri and Maha Shivaratri. It's considered "vrat" food, suitable for those following religious dietary restrictions.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

20 min.

serving

5 servings

instructions

step 1

Rinse the sabudana in cold water until the water runs clear. Drain the excess water.

step 2

Place the rinsed sabudana in a bowl and add just enough water to cover it. Let it soak for 4-5 hours or until the pearls become soft and can be easily mashed between your fingers. You can also soak them overnight for best results.

step 3

Heat oil in a pan or skillet over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and asafoetida. Sauté for a few seconds until they sizzle. Add the diced potatoes and cook until they turn slightly golden and crispy. Add the chopped green chilies and sauté for a minute to infuse the spice. Now, add the soaked and drained sabudana to the pan. Mix well with the other ingredients.

step 4

Cook on low to medium heat, stirring occasionally. Sabudana will turn translucent, indicating it's cooked. This usually takes about 5-7 minutes. Add crushed roasted peanuts and salt to taste. Mix well and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.

step 5

Remove from heat. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice if desired. Your Sabudana Khichdi is ready to serve. Enjoy this delightful and quick dish, often served during fasting or as a snack or breakfast option.

ingredients

1 cup sabudana (tapioca pearls)

1/2 cup roasted peanuts, coarsely crushed

2-3 green chilies, finely chopped

1 medium-sized potato, boiled and diced

1 teaspoon cumin seeds

A pinch of asafoetida (hing)

Salt to taste

1 large onion, thinly sliced

2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise

2 tablespoons oil
