Bread Upama

Bread upma is a popular and easy-to-make South Indian dish that repurposes leftover bread into a flavorful and satisfying breakfast or snack. It's a versatile and quick recipe that's perfect for using up stale bread. One of the most appealing aspects of bread upma is its ability to transform leftover bread into a delicious and savory dish. Whether you have excess bread from the previous day or want to breathe new life into slightly stale slices, bread upma is an excellent choice.

prep time

17 min.

cook time

11 min.

serving

2 servings

instructions

step 1

Heat oil in a pan or skillet over medium heat. Add mustard seeds, urad dal, and cumin seeds. Let them splutter and become fragrant. Add finely chopped onions, slit green chilies, and curry leaves. Saut√© until the onions become translucent.

step 2

Add the torn or cut bread pieces to the pan. Sprinkle turmeric powder and salt over the bread. Gently toss and stir the bread pieces in the pan, ensuring they are well-coated with the seasoned oil mixture. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the bread starts to crisp up slightly.

step 3

Remove from heat. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves if desired. Your Bread Upma is ready to serve hot. Enjoy it as a quick and delicious breakfast or snack.

ingredients

4-5 slices of bread, torn or cut into bite-sized pieces

1 small onion, finely chopped

1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds

8-10 curry leaves

1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder

1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds

Salt to taste
