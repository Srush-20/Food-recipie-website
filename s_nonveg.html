Chicken Biryani

Chicken biryani is a beloved and iconic Indian dish renowned for its aromatic flavors, tender chicken, and fragrant rice. It represents the essence of Indian cuisine and has variations across the country. Chicken biryani is celebrated for its captivating aromas and complex flavors. It features a blend of aromatic spices such as cumin, coriander, cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon, which infuse the dish with a rich and enticing fragrance. The interplay of these spices creates a multi-layered taste experience that is both comforting and exciting.

prep time

50 min.

cook time

20 min.

instructions

step 1

In a bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, salt, and lemon juice. Add chicken pieces, coat them well, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (or overnight for better flavor).

step 2

Rinse Basmati rice until water runs clear. Soak rice in water for 30 minutes. In a large pot, bring water to a boil, add soaked rice, green cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and salt. Cook until rice is 70% done, then drain and set aside.

step 3

In a large pot, heat oil or ghee over medium heat. Add sliced onions and cook until golden and crispy. Remove half for garnish.

step 4

To the pot with onions, add cumin seeds, bay leaf, and green chilies. Saut√© briefly, then add marinated chicken. Cook until it's no longer pink and begins to turn golden

step 5

Layer the partially cooked rice evenly over the chicken. Sprinkle half of the chopped coriander and mint leaves. Optionally, drizzle saffron-infused milk for fragrance and color.

step 6

Cover the pot tightly with a lid or foil. Reduce heat to low and let it cook for 20-25 minutes. Remove from heat and rest, covered, for 10 minutes. Fluff with a fork, garnish with fried onions, coriander, and mint leaves. Serve hot.

1.5 cups Basmati rice Water for cooking rice
